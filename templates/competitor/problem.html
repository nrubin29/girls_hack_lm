Tic-Tac-Toe

Welcome to this year’s virtual girls.hack(“LM”)! Today you will work with your team to code a unique and creative version of a popular game. Starter code will be provided to you in python or java so that you can work in the coding language that you are comfortable with.

You and your team members will be coding tic-tac-toe. You can do as much as you want with this, stopping at just the original game, or adding some kind of variation to this childhood classic. Some examples include: Nine Men’s Morris, Ultimate tic-tac-toe, and Sequence. Your team will decide how to go about this, whether your solution be point-click with AI (Artificial Intelligence), a Choose-Your-Own-Adventure (Text-Based) game, or some other version. Your work must be submitted by 5 o’clock.

Within the comments of the starter code, you will find information and details describing the individual sections. Additionally, there will be different student-led lessons throughout the day going through the starter code and explaining coding techniques such as AI, graphics, and arrays. These lessons are meant to help you succeed in the project no matter your current computer science level, so please take advantage of these if you feel you would benefit from them. Lastly, there will be a help desk available at all times if you encounter any problems, need help troubleshooting, or have any general coding or competition questions. At the end of the competition, your program will be judged on creativity and functionality. So make sure to brainstorm some crazy ideas in the beginning.

Happy Coding!
